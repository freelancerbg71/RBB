When you buy a physical scratchcard at a gas station, there is a built-in speed limit. You hand over cash, get a coin, and spend a minute physically scratching away the silver latex. You might buy three of them and sip a coffee while you play. 

Online casinos looked at that process and removed everything except the transaction. 

Online Scratchcards (often labeled as "Instant Win" games) are the purest, fastest, and most fast-paced form of gambling on the internet. They offer simple rules with zero required strategy. However, because the speed of play is exceptionally high, understanding how this pacing impacts your bankroll is crucial for enjoying the game responsibly.

## The Mechanics of the 'Scratch'

When you load up an online scratchcard, you will see a digital replica of a physical card. You buy the card, then use your mouse or finger to "swipe" over the hidden symbols to reveal them one by one. 

<MythBuster myth="If I scratch the corners first, or stop scratching right before revealing the final symbol, it affects the outcome of the game." reality="THE MATH CHECK">
The scratching action is a visual representation. It is a cosmetic animation layered on top of a basic Random Number Generator (RNG). The exact millisecond you click "Buy Card", the server securely dictates whether you won or lost, and exactly how much. You could unplug your computer before you even start scratching, and the money would still be mathematically added (or removed) from your casino balance. The thrill comes entirely from the momentary suspense of the reveal, as the mathematics have already securely resolved the outcome.
</MythBuster>

## The 'Reveal All' Feature

Because developers know the scratching animation gets boring for high-volume players, they include a button labeled **"Reveal All"**. 

<StepNode step="1" title="The Purchase">
You select a stake (e.g., $5.00) and click "Buy."
</StepNode>

<StepNode step="2" title="The Instant Result">
Instead of swiping your mouse, you click "Reveal All". In an fraction of a second, the entire card is cleared. A "You Won $0.00" message flashes. 
</StepNode>

<StepNode step="3" title="The Loop">
Before you can even register the loss, the "Buy New Card" button lights up, perfectly positioned right where your mouse cursor is already resting.
</StepNode>

This creates a high-speed behavioral loop. You can easily click through twenty $5.00 scratchcards in under 60 seconds. That is $100 wagered in a minute, on a game with zero entertainment value beyond the instantaneous flash of a win/loss screen. 

## The RTP Reality

Scratchcards generally have an RTP (Return to Player) that hovers between **90% and 95%**. 

While this is significantly better than the abysmal 60-70% RTP offered by state-run lottery physical scratchcards, it is still worse than most modern slot machines (96%) and much worse than table games like Blackjack or Baccarat (99%). 

Players effectively accept a higher house edge in exchange for the convenience of instant results. Like many highly volatile slots, the paytable often balances frequent small returns with the rare chance of a substantial multiplier.
